How to Set Up a Custom Domain Email

A Step By Step guide To Setting up a Custom Domain Email for Free

November 7, 2019 - 2 minute read -
Email Gmail

In this example I will use NameSilo. Having the cheepest domains on the market I prefer NameSilo.

Lets dive in...

What you will need:

  1. A Gmail account
  2. A Domain

Step 1: Set Up Email Forwarding

At the top right corner click Manage My Domains

Click on your domain name

Scroll down to DNS Records

Click Update

Scroll down to Email Forwarding

Click Apply Template

At the top right corner click Manage My Domains

Click Email Forwarding

Enter an [astrisk] into the first field

In the next enter the email you want it to forward to. (e.g. abcdefghijklmnop@gmail.com)

It may take up to 48 hours for your DNS changes to take effect. You can test your new email by sending an email to [anything]@[yourdomain.com]

Step 2: Setup Gmail to send custom domain emails

Go to gmail.com

Sign In to your gmail account

Click on your avatar

Click Google Account

On the sidebar click Security

Note: 2-Step Verification needs to be on

Click App Passwords

Select Mail

In the next field select Other

Enter the domain name you are using for you email

Click Generate

Copy the password. You will need it in the next few steps

Click on the settings icon

Click settings

Click Accounts and Imports

Click Add another Email address

Enter the name of the email address

Enter the email you want to send emails as

In the input field enter smtp.gmail.com

Enter your gmail username

Paste the app password you copied (e.g. qdlw jpms hgsd svzh)

Select port 365

Click Add Account


You will recieve an email giving you a verification code. Paste that code into the input box. Now Click verify. You are all done! you can now send custom domain emails using gmail! When sending an email click the down arrow in the From: section, then select your custom domain email address.
